# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

General public — families, history buffs, and casual visitors arriving around July 4, 2026 for America's 250th anniversary (the Semiquincentennial). They're on phones and desktops, in a celebratory mood, with a few minutes to scroll. The job: feel the sweep of 250 years of American history and come away moved, informed, and proud.

## Product Purpose

A single-page celebration site telling America's story from 1776 to 2026 — its history era by era, plus what makes America distinctive: invention, industry, and its place in the world. Success = visitors scroll the full journey, linger on the interactive moments, and share the link. Deployed to Vercel for public visiting.

## Brand Personality

Sweeping, crafted, celebratory. Like a national-museum exhibition designed by a great poster artist — grand but human. Emotional goals: awe at the scale of the journey, warmth, optimism.

## Anti-references

- Government-website blandness (usa.gov, .gov template beige)
- Cheap clip-art patriotism: waving-flag GIFs, eagle stock photos, red-white-blue confetti everywhere
- Generic AI landing page: identical card grids, gradient text, eyebrow labels on every section
- Textbook dryness — this is a birthday, not a term paper

## Design Principles

1. **The design is the timeline.** The site's own art direction evolves era by era — parchment engraving for 1776, wood-type letterpress for the 1800s, industrial poster for the 1900s, crisp digital for today. Form tells the story alongside content.
2. **Celebration with substance.** Every visual flourish carries a real fact, date, or story. Spectacle earns its place with content.
3. **One idea per fold.** Long scroll, deliberate pacing, single dominant moment per viewport.
4. **Red, white, and blue by suggestion, not saturation.** The palette shifts with the eras; the national colors appear as deliberate accents, never as wallpaper.
5. **Built like it matters.** Navigation on Meta's Astryx design system; smooth motion; fast; accessible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for all scroll and entrance animation. Audio strictly opt-in with a visible toggle. Semantic landmarks and keyboard-navigable era navigation. Contrast ≥4.5:1 for body text in every era's palette.
